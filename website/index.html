Thesis

Useful intelligence should not require every thought to travel to the largest model, the farthest data center, or the most expensive machine.

A meaningful portion of inference may be handled by smaller models, cached knowledge, existing context, and idle capacity distributed across ordinary devices. The laboratory investigates where that boundary lies, how it changes, and what people gain when more intelligence remains near them.

Our central question is not how little frontier compute we can use. It is how much useful capability we can place within human reach without compromising quality, safety, or choice.

Research principles

Technical progress is incomplete when people lose agency.

  1. 01
    Keep people in control

    Automation should expand judgment, not quietly replace it.

  2. 02
    Keep sensitive context close

    Locality can be a privacy boundary, not only a performance choice.

  3. 03
    Measure before claiming

    Every idea must survive contact with observable evidence.

  4. 04
    Publish what did not work

    A careful negative result still narrows the search space.

  5. 05
    Spend powerful compute deliberately

    Capability, cost, energy, and consequence belong in the same decision.
